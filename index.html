Tribute to Swami Vivekananda

Introduction

Swami Vivekananda (1863 - 1902) was a renowned Hindu monk and a key figure in the introduction of Indian philosophies of Vedanta and Yoga to the Western world. He was a disciple of Sri Ramakrishna Paramahamsa and played a significant role in the revitalization of Hinduism in India during the 19th century.

Chicago Address

One of Swami Vivekananda's most iconic moments was his speech at the Parliament of the World's Religions in Chicago in 1893. In this historic address, he introduced Hinduism and its principles to the Western audience, receiving a standing ovation for his message of religious tolerance and universal brotherhood.

Inspirational Quotes

"Arise, awake, and stop not till the goal is reached."
"The greatest religion is to be true to your own nature. Have faith in yourselves!"

Legacy

Swami Vivekananda's teachings continue to inspire millions of people worldwide. His emphasis on self-realization, service to humanity, and the unity of all religions remains relevant to this day. He founded the Ramakrishna Mission and Ramakrishna Math to carry on his work, promoting education, healthcare, and social welfare.

Passing

Swami Vivekananda attained Mahasamadhi on July 4, 1902, at the young age of 39. Although his physical existence was brief, his impact on the world and the spiritual realm is immeasurable.

Conclusion

Swami Vivekananda's teachings continue to guide and uplift people across the globe. His message of universal love, harmony, and spiritual realization resonates with people of all backgrounds, making him a timeless and revered figure in the history of humanity.
